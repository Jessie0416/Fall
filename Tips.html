At the Beginning

1. Learn the Rules

Photography rules are essential because they provide a foundation for more advanced photography tips and tricks.

2. Expose the Focus

A blurry picture is unusable, so you should always focus on and properly expose for the subject before adjusting the frame.

3. Focus on the eyes

Eyes are a natural focal point.When taking portrait photographs at any aperture, make sure you focus on the eyes

4. Learn from the Mistakes

Turning mistakes into lessons that build your skills.try a technique you haven’t done before and expect to make many mistakes along the way.

5. Perfect the Exposure

Balancing and understanding the relationships between shutter speed, aperture, and ISO settings for a proper exposure in photography.

6. Always be ready

Keep your camera on one of the semi-auto or full automatic modes for unexpected pictures before your subject flies, drives, or runs away.

While Photographing

1. Use a Wider Aperture

Aim for an aperture size around f/2.8 to f/5.6 to make the background behind your subject more blurred out. This will help remove distracting backgrounds

2. No Blurry Pictures

Matching shutter speed to the lens focal length. Longer lenses are heavier and more difficult to keep steady — making the shutter speed faster avoids camera shake.

3. Straighten and Crop

Straightening shots by looking through your camera’s viewfinder before capturing an image, but it’s not always easy to get this perfect on the first try.

4. Avoid Camera Shake

Using both hands to hold your camera. Increasing ISO and aperture for quicker shutter speeds and less blurry images.

5. Keep Both Eyes Open

keeping both eyes open so you can predict when your subject will enter the frame, whcih is important for capturing action.

6. Use Exposure

Sometimes you’ll take photographs that are too bright or too dark.You can fix image by using exposure compensation.

Portrait

1. Portrait Photography

Use window light. Turn off all the lights in the room and move near a window with some curtains so you can play around with diffusing the light.

2. Pet Photography

Shoot in shutter priority mode and hover around 1/125 sec to 1/500 sec depending on the pet. Lastly, similar to human subjects keep focus on the eyes sharp.

3. Landscape Photography

Visualize the image you’re trying to achieve (where and when).Choose a proper lens and Use an aperture around f/16 or f/22 to achieve this.

4. Party Photography

Choose a wide zoom lens, with the widest range being about 24mm. Avoid using the built-in flash and opt for an external flash.

5. Paint with Light

Find a dark room or wait until after the sun sets and turn off any nearby lights. Set your camera to the lowest ISO, usually 100 or 200.

6. Photograph Fireworks

Set your camera to ISO 100.Press the shutter release right before the fireworks go off and release the shutter once they fade.
